About

One of the most important features of search engines is that they scrape the entire web world. This scraped data is then processed and saved and presented to the user in the most appropriate form. However, the fact that the web world today is very large and there is a lot of unnecessary and misleading information makes this job difficult.
Therefore, dividing the problems into small pieces and moving forward makes things more logical and easier. A company search engine is one of these small pieces.
The Company Search Engine project aims to develop a powerful and useful vertical search engine designed to facilitate and accelerate the process of finding relevant company information.
One of the main issues addressed is the inefficiency of existing search solutions in retrieving comprehensive and accurate company data and the inaccuracy that occurs due to this inefficiency.
As a result of these problems, I planned to develop a vertical search engine that better meets users' requests and effectively crawls web pages, extracts meaningful keywords, associates pages with user queries and calculates their relevance.
While developing the Company Search Engine, I used the ranking methodology, which is the basis of search engines, to determine the relationship, similarity and compatibility between the query searched, the data stored in the database and the data of the crawled web pages, I used the BM25 algorithm, one of the ranking algorithms, in my project., and the Named Entity Recognition (NER) technique, a form of natural language processing (NLP), to ensure that the results obtained are for the purpose of the search engine. Another popular technique I used in my project is the TF-IDF algorithm used in text similarity scoring.
When I consider the results, I can see that they worked. As good as the expected results are, I think that there are still shortcomings.
Perhaps the future should include expanding the dataset, improving the algorithms and integrating machine learning models to further improve the search capabilities.
